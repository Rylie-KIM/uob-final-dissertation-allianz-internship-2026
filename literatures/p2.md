## 🇰🇷 한국어 요약

### 1. 문제 정의 — "Runaway Feedback Loop"

PredPol 같은 예측적 경찰 배치 모델은 다음 사이클로 작동:

```
과거 범죄 incident 데이터 → 모델 학습 → 우선 순찰 지역 예측 →
경찰 배치 → "discovered" incident 수집 → 학습 데이터에 다시 투입
        ↑___________________________________________________|
```

이 구조에서 발생하는 핵심 문제:

- **Discovered incident (d_A):** 경찰이 그 지역에 있어야만 보이는 범죄 — 배치 결정에 **영향받음**.
- **Reported incident (r_A):** 주민이 911 등으로 신고 — 배치 결정에 **영향받지 않음**.

PredPol은 두 가지를 가중치 (w_d, w_r) 로 섞어 학습하는데, **w_d 가 크면 (= discovered 비중이 높으면)** 모델은 자기가 보낸 곳에서만 데이터를 받으므로 그 지역의 추정 범죄율이 인위적으로 부풀려진다. → 다음 사이클에 또 그 지역 → 무한 가속. 이것이 **"runaway feedback loop"**.

**보험과의 직접 대응:**

| 예측적 경찰 | 보험 사기 탐지 (Allianz) |
|------------|-------------------------|
| Patrol 배치 결정 | Investigation 배치 결정 |
| Discovered crime | Confirmed fraud (조사관이 확인한 것) |
| Reported crime (911) | Customer complaint / whistleblower tip |
| 같은 동네 반복 출동 | 같은 segment 반복 조사 |
| Crime rate λ_A | True fraud rate λ_A |

### 2. 핵심 기술 — Pólya Urn Model

PredPol의 동역학을 **Pólya 항아리 (urn) 모델**로 형식화:

- 빨간 공 = 지역 A에서 범죄 발견, 검은 공 = 지역 B에서 범죄 발견.
- 매 스텝: 공 하나 뽑기 → 색 보고 → 같은 색 공 + α 개 추가.
- 비유: 경찰을 A에 더 자주 보낼수록, A에서 더 많이 발견되고, A 공이 항아리에 쌓임.

#### 2-1. 균일 범죄율 (λ_A = λ_B)

- **Lemma 2 (Renlund 2010):** 표준 Pólya urn은 Beta(n_r, n_b) 분포로 수렴.
- 즉 **초기 prior** 에 따라 임의의 분포로 수렴하지, 진짜 비율(50:50)로 가지 않는다.
- → 균일 범죄율인데도 모델은 한 지역에 편중 가능 (= 사실상 불공정).

#### 2-2. 비균일 범죄율 (λ_A ≠ λ_B) — 가장 충격적 결과

- **Lemma 4:** Discovered-only 데이터로 학습하면, urn 은 **확률 1로** 더 큰 λ 쪽으로 100% 수렴.
- 예: λ_A = 0.10, λ_B = 0.11 → 1% 차이임에도 경찰은 결국 B에 100% 배치.
- 이를 논문은 **"go with the winner"** 라 부른다 — 작은 격차가 완전 집중으로 증폭.

> ⚠️ Allianz 관점에서 핵심: **fraud rate 가 segment 간 살짝만 달라도, SFP loop 는 조사 인력을 한 segment 에 100% 몰아준다.** 이것은 단순한 모델 결함이 아니라 **수학적 필연** (Lemma 4 가 증명).

#### 2-3. Reported incident 을 포함하면?

- Discovered + reported 가중합 (Section 3.4) → urn 의 limiting fraction 은 quadratic polynomial 의 근으로 결정.
- 결과: reported 만으로 feedback 이 사라지려면 (a) w_d 가 매우 작거나, (b) λ_A ≈ λ_B 여야 함.
- **하지만** 정확히 λ 가 다를 때가 예측이 필요한 상황이므로, reported 데이터를 섞는 것만으론 부족.

### 3. 제안 — Black-box Improvement Policy

핵심 발상: PredPol 알고리즘 자체를 바꾸지 말고, **입력 데이터를 필터링**하자.

#### 3-1. Discovered-only 의 경우 (Section 3.5.1)

수정된 룰: 공을 뽑은 후, 그 색 그대로 추가하지 말고,
- **항아리에서 다시 한 번 공을 뽑아본다.**
- 두 색이 **같으면** 새 공을 추가하지 **않는다.**
- 두 색이 **다르면** 새 공을 추가한다.

수식적으로 A-색 공이 추가될 확률이 `λ_A · (n_A / (n_A + n_B))` → `λ_A · (n_B / (n_A + n_B))` 로 바뀜.

**의미:** 이는 **rejection sampling** 의 변형이며, **Thompson-Horvitz 추정기**의 변형 (불균등 표집 보정). 직관: "이 지역에 자주 보내질수록, 그 지역에서 발견한 incident 은 더 큰 비율로 버린다."

#### 3-2. Discovered + Reported 의 경우 (Section 3.5.2)

- Reported incident 은 그대로 두고, **discovered 부분에만** 위 보정을 적용.
- 식: `p = r_O / (r_A + r_B)`, r_O = 오늘 *순찰하지 않은* 지역의 예측율.
- 한 문장 요약: **"경찰을 보낼 확률이 높은 지역일수록, 그 지역의 discovered incident 은 학습 데이터에서 더 많이 제외한다."**

### 4. 실험 — Lum & Isaac (2016) Oakland 마약 데이터

- **Top1 vs Top2:** 진짜 비율 56.7 % (Top1 의 비중)
- **Top1 vs Random:** 진짜 비율 61.0 %

**Figure 1 결과:**

| 입력 | Without improvement | With improvement |
|------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Discovered only | **100% Top1** (완전 폭주) | 진짜 비율 56.7 / 61 % 수렴 ✅ |
| All incidents (w_d = w_r = 0.5) | 잘못된 비율 수렴 | 진짜 비율 수렴 ✅ |

**Figure 2:** PredPol 자체를 시뮬레이션 — improvement policy 를 입력에 적용하면 PredPol 의 출력 비율이 진짜 범죄율 근처로 수렴 (여전히 noisy 하지만 unbiased).

### 6. 한계 (논문이 명시 + Allianz 관점에서 추가)

논문이 명시:

- 2-region 단순화 — 실제는 다지역, multi-product.
- Urn 은 **demographics, time-of-day 같은 rich features 를 모델링하지 않음** — PredPol 의 abstraction 만 캡쳐.
- 핵심 가정: *"reported / discovered rate 이 true rate 을 tracking 한다"*. Bialik (2016) 등 증거는 신고 자체가 area / crime type 에 따라 편향됨을 보임.

Allianz 관점에서 추가로 신경 써야 할 점:

- 보험에서 **"reported fraud"** 도 편향됨 — 어떤 고객층은 신고하지 않음, broker 채널은 신고 경로가 다름. 즉 r_A 도 unbiased 가 아님.
- **Multi-product line** 환경 — 2-color urn 을 N-color 로 확장하면 수렴 성질이 바뀔 수 있음 (논문도 future work 로 언급).
- **FCA / Consumer Duty 관점:** "go with the winner" 효과는 동등한 fraud rate 인 두 인구집단에 대해 불공정한 조사 빈도를 야기 → 직접적인 fairness 위반.

## 🇬🇧 English Summary

### 1. The Problem — "Runaway Feedback Loops"

Predictive policing systems such as **PredPol** operate in a cycle:

```
Historical incident data → train model → predict high-risk areas →
deploy police → record "discovered" incidents → feed back into training
        ↑__________________________________________________|
```

Two crime-data types behave very differently inside this cycle:

- **Discovered incidents (d_A)** — observed only because police are present → **endogenous** to the deployment decision.
- **Reported incidents (r_A)** — phoned in by residents (e.g., 911) → **exogenous** to deployment.

PredPol mixes them with weights (w_d, w_r). When **w_d is large**, the model is trained on signals it itself generated, inflating estimated crime in over-policed neighbourhoods and re-deploying police there next cycle. This is the **runaway feedback loop**.

**Direct insurance analogy:**

| Predictive policing | Insurance fraud detection (Allianz) |
|---|---|
| Patrol allocation | Investigation allocation |
| Discovered crime | Confirmed fraud (label seen by investigator) |
| Reported crime (911) | Customer complaint / whistleblower tip |
| Repeated patrols in same neighbourhood | Repeated investigations in same segment |
| Crime rate λ_A | True fraud rate λ_A |

### 2. Core Technique — Pólya Urn Model

The paper formalises PredPol's dynamics as a **generalised Pólya urn**:

- Red ball = an incident discovered in region A; black = region B.
- Each step: draw a ball, observe its colour, replace it + add more of the same colour.
- Intuition: more frequent visits to A → more discoveries in A → more A-balls in the urn → still more visits.

#### 2-1. Uniform crime rates (λ_A = λ_B)

- **Lemma 2 (Renlund, 2010):** The standard Pólya urn converges to **Beta(n_r, n_b)**.
- The limit depends on the **initial prior**, not on the true 50:50 split.
- ⇒ Even with identical underlying crime rates, the system can converge to skewed policing.

#### 2-2. Non-uniform crime rates (λ_A ≠ λ_B) — the headline result

- **Lemma 4:** With discovered-only data, the urn converges (**almost surely**) to **100 %** on whichever region has the higher λ.
- Example: λ_A = 0.10, λ_B = 0.11 → only a 1 percentage-point gap, yet policing converges to 100 % on B.
- The paper calls this **"go with the winner"** — small differentials amplify to full concentration.

> ⚠️ Allianz takeaway: **even a marginal fraud-rate gap between segments causes the SFP loop to allocate 100 % of investigative resources to one segment.** This is not a model defect — it is a mathematical inevitability under the loop's dynamics.

#### 2-3. What if we add reported incidents?

- With a weighted mix of discovered + reported (Section 3.4), the urn's limiting fraction is a root of a quadratic polynomial.
- Feedback vanishes only if (a) w_d is tiny, or (b) λ_A ≈ λ_B.
- **But** the interesting cases — where prediction is useful — are precisely those with different rates, so simply blending in reports does not solve it.

### 3. Proposed Fix — Black-box Improvement Policy

Key idea: don't change PredPol itself, **filter its inputs**.

#### 3-1. Discovered-only case (§3.5.1)

Modified rule: after drawing a ball,
- **Re-sample another ball** from the urn.
- If the two colours **match**, do **not** add a new ball.
- If they **differ**, add the new ball.

This changes the probability of adding an A-ball from `λ_A · n_A/(n_A+n_B)` to `λ_A · n_B/(n_A+n_B)`. Crucially, the marginal addition rate is now **proportional to λ_A**, restoring an unbiased update.

**Mechanism:** this is a form of **rejection sampling** and is equivalent to a **Thompson–Horvitz** style correction for unequal sampling probabilities. The intuition: *the more often we send police to a region, the more aggressively we discount incidents discovered there*.

#### 3-2. Mixed discovered + reported (§3.5.2)

- Leave reported incidents alone.
- Apply the correction **only to discovered incidents**, with `p = r_O / (r_A + r_B)`, where r_O is the predicted rate of the district **not** policed that day.
- One-line summary: **"the more likely we are to send police to a district, the less likely we should incorporate the incidents discovered there."**

### 4. Experiments — Lum & Isaac (2016) Oakland Drug-Crime Data

- **Top1 vs Top2:** ground-truth share for Top1 = 56.7 %
- **Top1 vs Random:** ground-truth share for Top1 = 61.0 %

**Figure 1 — headline:**

| Input | Without improvement | With improvement |
|---|---|---|
| Discovered only | Converges to **100 %** in Top1 (full runaway) | Converges to true share (56.7 / 61 %) ✅ |
| All incidents (w_d = w_r = 0.5) | Converges to wrong rate | Converges to true rate ✅ |

**Figure 2:** Simulating PredPol itself — when the improvement policy is applied to its inputs, PredPol's deployment converges around the true crime rate (still noisy, but unbiased).

### 5. Direct Relevance to the Allianz SFP Project

If P1 anchors the **evaluation** workstream, P2 anchors the **simulation and mitigation** workstreams.

| Allianz phase | P2's direct contribution |
|---|---|
| **Build 01 SFP Simulation** | The Pólya urn translates directly to fraud detection — red = fraud discovered in segment A, black = segment B. Lemma 4 supplies the mathematical justification of *why* the SFP loop forms. |
| **Build 02 Loop Detection** | A "go-with-the-winner" signature — investigation share converging monotonically toward one segment over time — is a strong candidate diagnostic for SFP suspicion. |
| **Build 04 Intervention Analysis** | Pre/post comparison of an improvement policy applied as a natural experiment is a DiD setup. |
| **Build 05 Randomisation Strategy** | **The paper's fix *is* a randomisation strategy** (rejection sampling). It is the natural baseline against which ε-greedy or Thompson sampling can be benchmarked. |
| **Build 06 Causal Mitigation** | Black-box input correction — applicable even when Allianz cannot retouch the production model. Regulator-friendly. |

**Concrete mapping:**

- λ_A, λ_B = true fraud rates by product line (motor / home / pet / commercial)
- n_A, n_B = cumulative investigations to date in each line
- w_d = share of fraud discovered through model-driven investigations
- w_r = share discovered through customer complaints / whistleblower channels (typically small)

**P1 + P2 together form a complete pipeline:**

1. **P1 Contraction** answers *"how good is the current model, unbiased?"* (retrospective evaluation under selective labels).
2. **P2 Urn + rejection sampling** answers *"how will the loop unfold from here, and what input edit prevents it?"* (prospective simulation and mitigation).

### 6. Limitations

**Acknowledged in the paper:**

- Two-region simplification — real deployments span many areas and product lines.
- The urn ignores **rich features** (demographics, time of day) — it captures PredPol's abstraction, not real predictive systems.
- The fix assumes **reported and discovered rates track true rates**. Evidence (Bialik 2016) shows that even reported rates are biased by area and crime type.

**Additional concerns from the Allianz angle:**

- In insurance, **reported fraud is also biased** — some customer segments under-report, and broker-channel cases follow different reporting paths. So r_A is not unbiased either.
- **Multi-product / multi-line** environments need an N-colour generalisation of the urn — the paper explicitly flags this as future work.
- **FCA / Consumer Duty implication:** the "go with the winner" effect produces unequal investigation rates even between demographic groups with identical fraud rates — a direct fairness concern, not just an efficiency one.
